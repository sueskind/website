Worksteps

The original image was a normal leopard which I turned grayscale to make it look like a snowleopard.
After masking it out from the background, I used different smoke-formed brushes to paint the smoke effect.
Last I added lensflares.

Worksteps

I took a picture of an owl and drastically increased contrast.
Then cut out the eyes from a duplicate layer and put a colorful galaxy picture in between.
I finished by adding a vignette and a rainbow lensflare (because subtile lensflares make everything better).

Worksteps

We made this photo of my friend @aaron_partecke in front of a white wall in my basement, so I had to blur the wall texture a bit. After some minor retouching I created the feeling of this picture by increasing the contrast of only the green color channel.
Finally it got a vignette and white bars to be in instagram-square-shape.

This one was a spontaneous shot in the forest with a setting sun shining frontally on the face, trying not to get blind by staring into the sun. After picking the best try with the right eyelash shadows, I retouched the skin, brightened the eye and increased the contrast.

Worksteps

I saw this cat sitting in a corn field, probably waiting for some prey. It was curious about what I was doing, so it looked at me and I could get this frontal shot.
In Photoshop I added the scratches and enlarged lensflares and increased the contrast of each color channel seperately to make the colors more vibrant.

I created my Instgram name with a cool font I found in Cinema 4D and duplicated the layer. Then I used a plug-in to shatter the copy and place it a little bit in the back. After exporting I added the background and smoke in Photoshop and gave it a greenish look.
